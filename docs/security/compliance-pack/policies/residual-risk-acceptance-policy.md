# Residual Risk Acceptance Policy

## Purpose

Define when and how residual security risk can be accepted temporarily.

## Principles

- Residual risk acceptance is exceptional and time-bound.
- Acceptance does not replace remediation.
- Compensating controls are mandatory.

## Mandatory requirements

- Named owner and named reviewer (different people).
- Risk score documented (likelihood x impact).
- Clear reason why immediate remediation is not feasible.
- Compensating controls documented and active.
- Explicit expiry date (<= 90 days from acceptance).
- Approval reference recorded (CTO + Security Lead for high/critical contexts).
- Linked evidence artifact.

## Prohibited cases

- Acceptance without expiry date.
- Acceptance without compensating controls.
- Silent acceptance outside the registered log.

## Governance

- All active accepted risks are reviewed weekly.
- Expired accepted risks are escalated and cannot remain active.
- Closure requires mitigation evidence and reviewer confirmation.
